Late Filing Penalty & How to Fix It
The Problem
⚠ Late Filing Penalty $600

The IRS charges 5% of unpaid tax per month for filing late (up to 25%). With ≈$2,500 owed and 4–5 months late, $600 is about right.

Your CPA filed late — this is on them, not you.

Call IRS: 1-800-829-1040
Say: "I'd like to request a First-Time Penalty Abatement"
First-Time Abatement (FTA) — How It Works
1
Check Eligibility
No penalties in the past 3 years, all returns filed, and tax is paid or in a payment arrangement.
2
Call or Write the IRS
Phone is fastest — approved on the same call. Use Form 843 by mail if the penalty is already paid.
3
$600 Penalty Wiped
Covers failure-to-file and failure-to-pay. One-time use per taxpayer.
4
Resets After 3 Clean Years
File on time and pay what you owe — eligible again after 2025, 2026, and 2027.

Your Questions, Answered
Should I use the FTA waiver to get rid of the $600 penalty my CPA caused?
Short Answer: Yes — 100%

The FTA waiver exists precisely for this situation: a taxpayer with a clean history hit with a penalty that wasn't their fault. Using it is legitimate, IRS-sanctioned, and has zero downside.

Why you should use it:

  • The penalty happened because your CPA filed late, not you
  • It's a one-time use — this is exactly the moment it was designed for
  • Worst case if you ask: they say no. There's no penalty for requesting
  • $600 back for a 10-minute phone call

Ideally your CPA makes this call since the late filing was their error. Ask: "Can you call the IRS and request the FTA for the late filing penalty this week?"

Call 1-800-829-1040 and say: "I'd like to request a First-Time Penalty Abatement for tax year [year]. I have a clean penalty history for the prior three years."

How do I approach my CPA so they take responsibility and reduce my workload?
CPA Accountability

Be direct, factual, and calm. You're not asking a favor — you're asking them to fix their mistake.

Use this script:

"Hey [name], the late filing generated a $600 IRS penalty. Since the delay was on the filing side, I'd like you to call the IRS and request the First-Time Penalty Abatement on my behalf — that's a standard call any CPA can make. Can you handle that this week?"

To reduce your workload going forward:

  • Ask for a January document checklist — no back and forth
  • Ask them to reach out proactively before deadlines
  • Ask them to explain every line in plain English
  • Ask: "Are there credits or deductions I might be missing?"

If they refuse to fix the penalty — that's your sign to find a new CPA.

What are the signs of a good CPA, and how do I find one?
Finding a Great CPA

You need a proactive, communicative CPA — not necessarily the most expensive one.

Green flags:

  • Reaches out to you before deadlines
  • Explains things in plain English without being asked
  • Proactively mentions credits you didn't ask about (EITC, IRA, FTA)
  • Asks about life changes every year
  • Takes ownership when something goes wrong

Red flags:

  • Only contacts you once a year at tax time
  • Filed late and won't fix the penalty
  • Has never mentioned EITC, FTA, or IRA to you
  • Makes you feel rushed or confused

To find one: CPAverify.org verifies licenses. NATP.org has a directory. Ask friends at similar income levels. Expect $200–$400 for federal + state.
